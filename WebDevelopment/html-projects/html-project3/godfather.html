Godfather

Summary

Directed by Francis Ford Coppola | Based on Mario Puzo’s novel The Corleone family, one of New York’s most powerful mafia families, is led by Don Vito Corleone. His youngest son, Michael Corleone, a World War II hero, initially wants no part in the family business. However, when a rival gangster, Virgil "The Turk" Sollozzo, attempts to assassinate Vito after his request for protection in drug dealings is rejected, Michael is pulled into the underworld. Michael takes revenge by killing Sollozzo and a corrupt police captain, Mark McCluskey, forcing him to flee to Sicily. During his exile, he marries a local woman, Apollonia, but she is killed in a car bombing meant for him. Meanwhile, back in the U.S., Vito recovers and negotiates peace among the Five Families, but his eldest son, Sonny, is brutally gunned down. Michael returns home after Vito arranges his safe passage, taking over the family after Vito’s death. He orchestrates a ruthless series of assassinations against rival mafia heads, solidifying his power. In one chilling sequence, while standing as godfather at his nephew’s baptism, Michael orders the simultaneous executions of his enemies. His transformation into a ruthless mob boss is complete when he has his sister Connie’s husband, Carlo, killed for his betrayal. When Connie confronts Michael, his wife, Kay, watches in horror as Michael lies about the murder, and the door to his office is ominously closed in her face. Michael has become the new Godfather, fully embracing the role he once rejected.
