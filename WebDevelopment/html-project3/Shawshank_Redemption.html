Shawshank Redemption

Summary

Directed by Frank Darabont | Based on Stephen King's novella "Rita Hayworth and Shawshank Redemption" Andy Dufresne, a successful banker, is wrongly convicted of murdering his wife and her lover. Sentenced to two consecutive life terms at Shawshank State Penitentiary, he must endure the harsh realities of prison life. At first, Andy keeps to himself, but over time, he befriends Ellis "Red" Redding, a longtime inmate and smuggler. Despite the brutal conditions, Andy maintains his composure and intelligence. He gains the trust of Warden Samuel Norton and the corrupt prison guards by helping them launder money under the alias "Randall Stephens." In return, Andy is allowed to improve the prison library and help fellow inmates earn their GEDs. However, when a new inmate, Tommy Williams, arrives with evidence that could prove Andy’s innocence, the warden has him killed to keep Andy under his control. Realizing he will never be freed legally, Andy meticulously carries out a secret escape plan over 19 years. He digs a tunnel through his cell wall using a rock hammer, hiding the hole behind a poster of Rita Hayworth (later replaced by Marilyn Monroe and Raquel Welch). On a stormy night, he crawls through a sewage pipe and emerges free. Before escaping, Andy exposes the warden’s money-laundering scheme by sending incriminating evidence to a newspaper. The warden, fearing exposure, commits suicide. Red, paroled after 40 years, follows Andy’s instructions to find a buried box containing money and a letter inviting him to join Andy in Zihuatanejo, Mexico. The film ends with Red joyfully reuniting with his friend on the beach, symbolizing hope and redemption.
